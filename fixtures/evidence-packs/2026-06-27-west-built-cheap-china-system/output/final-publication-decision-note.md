# Final Publication Decision Note

## Decision

Do not publish the current draft as fully evidenced.

The evidence pack now supports a cautious article-development route, but it does not yet support a finished article with strong claims about direct consumer harm from the EU low-value parcel duty.

## Current status

Status: hold for rewrite or further evidence.

This means the article can move forward only in one of two ways:

1. rewrite the article as cautious TWIS analysis using only supported and clearly caveated claims; or
2. hold publication until stronger direct evidence is added.

## What can be said now

A revised article can safely say that:

- the article argues Western capitalism helped build the cheap China production system.
- Reuters reported the EU 3 euro low-value parcel duty point.
- the European Commission framed low-value e-commerce imports as a customs, fairness, compliance, and environmental issue.
- Guardian/IFS reporting supports the general point that price rises on cheaper goods can hit poorer households harder.
- Reuters reporting supports the general point that Shein and Temu appeal to lower-income bargain-seeking shoppers.
- Reuters tariff-incidence reporting supports the general point that tariff and customs-duty costs can be borne by domestic businesses and consumers.

## What cannot be said yet

The current evidence does not support saying that:

- the EU duty will definitely make poorer consumers pay more.
- the full duty will be passed directly to shoppers.
- UK poorer consumers depend on direct-from-China platforms.
- the exact price effect of the EU duty is known.
- the whole historic outsourcing argument is fully evidenced.
- the current article draft is ready for publication without revision.

## Editorial judgement

The article has a usable political shape.

The evidence is now enough to support the direction of the argument, but not enough to support its strongest possible version.

The safest editorial route is to rewrite the article around risk, pressure, and cost-shifting rather than certainty.

## Required wording discipline

Use wording such as:

- may.
- risks.
- suggests.
- helps explain.
- supports the concern that.
- raises the question of who pays.

Avoid wording such as:

- proves.
- will definitely.
- directly causes.
- all costs.
- poor consumers will pay more.
- fully evidenced.

## Final decision

Do not publish the current draft as fully evidenced.

Proceed only if the article is rewritten as cautious analysis or if further direct evidence is added for platform use, pass-through, consumer impact, and historic outsourcing.
