# Article Readiness Summary

## Current decision

Not ready for publication.

This pack is stronger than a pure article-method test, but it is still a controlled TWIS article/sandbox pack.

It now has enough evidence to show that the article has a plausible sourced shape. It does not yet have enough evidence to support a finished publication article.

## What is now supported

The pack can safely support these limited statements:

- The article argues that Western capitalism helped build the cheap China production system and that later restrictions or repricing can shift costs onto poorer consumers.
- Reuters reported that EU finance ministers agreed to a temporary 3 euro duty on low-value e-commerce parcels from July 1, 2026.
- The European Commission reported large volumes of low-value consignments entering the EU market and linked this to customs reform, market fairness, compliance pressure, and environmental concerns.
- Guardian reporting on IFS research supports the general consumer-impact point that price rises on cheaper goods can hit lower-income households harder.
- Reuters reporting supports the general platform-use point that Shein and Temu appeal to lower-income bargain-seeking shoppers.
- Reuters reporting on tariff incidence supports the general pass-through point that tariff and customs-duty costs can be borne by domestic firms and consumers.

## What remains unsupported

The pack does not yet prove:

- the full historic outsourcing argument.
- corporate motive across Western companies and investors.
- the exact pass-through rate of the EU low-value parcel duty.
- direct price changes on Shein, Temu, AliExpress, or similar platforms.
- UK low-income dependence on direct-from-China platforms.
- measured harm to poorer consumers from this specific EU policy.
- that the article is ready for publication.

## Editorial risk

Risk remains medium.

The pack now has multiple supporting context sources, but several important links are still inferential:

- policy decision to consumer price.
- consumer price to low-income household impact.
- platform use to poorer consumer reliance.
- historic outsourcing to present political responsibility.

Those links may be valid editorially, but they need clearer evidence before publication.

## Safe article wording now

The article can safely use cautious wording such as:

- reported.
- suggests.
- helps explain.
- supports the concern that.
- raises the risk that.
- gives context for.

The article should avoid stronger wording such as:

- proves.
- shows exactly.
- directly caused.
- will definitely make poor consumers pay more.
- all costs will be passed on.

## Next evidence needed

To move the article closer to publication, the next useful sources would be:

1. a primary Council or EU source confirming the temporary duty decision and timetable.
2. platform-specific evidence on customs, fees, delivery charges, or pricing after duty changes.
3. consumer data on who uses low-value direct-from-China platforms, ideally by income group.
4. evidence on pass-through for low-value parcel duties specifically, not tariffs in general.
5. clearer sourcing for the historic Western outsourcing and corporate-margin argument.

## Publication recommendation

Do not publish as fully evidenced yet.

The article can be developed further using this pack, but the published version should either:

- use careful caveated language matching the current evidence, or
- wait for more direct source records on platform use, pass-through, and low-income consumer impact.
