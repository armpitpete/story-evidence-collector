# The Politics of Calling People Ordinary Evidence Pack

This is the first controlled TWIS article/sandbox Evidence Pack.

It now uses two sources:

- TWIS draft article: `The politics of calling people ordinary`
- ONS: `Household Costs Indices for UK household groups: January to March 2026`

The pack tests whether Evidence Pack v1 can preserve a small proof trail around a TWIS editorial claim and one external public source.

It is not a full publication evidence pack.

It does not prove the whole political argument. It records the narrow article-method claim that praise for ordinary people should be tested against policy outcomes and material conditions, then adds one official household-cost source as a limited external test.

## Research question

Can the TWIS article claim that political praise for ordinary people should be judged against policy outcomes rather than tone alone?

## Scope

In scope:

- one TWIS article draft.
- one official ONS household-cost source.
- one narrow editorial-method claim.
- one external material-conditions claim.
- basic source authority mapping.
- basic timeline, denial-check, and human-review records.

Out of scope:

- proving any specific politician's motive.
- checking all public speeches that use the phrase ordinary people.
- proving every policy outcome mentioned in the article.
- treating this as a finished publication evidence pack.
- proving wages, housing supply, transport quality, workplace power, or public service performance.

## Good enough

This pack is good enough when it validates and clearly separates:

- what the article argues.
- what the external source can support.
- what the pack proves structurally.
- what the pack does not prove.
